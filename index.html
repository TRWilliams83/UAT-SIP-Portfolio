About Me

I’m a Digital Maker and Fabrication student focused on turning ideas into working prototypes through iterative design and testing. My work centers on CAD-driven development in Fusion 360, rapid prototyping with a Bambu P1P 3D printer, and building functional self sustaining systems or replacements. Much of my recent work involves closed-loop environmental systems, where geometry and material behavior—capillary action, evaporation, and condensation—are treated as part of the engineering process. I approach projects like small experiments: design, build, measure what actually happens, then refine the design based on those results. Through my studies I have learned both patience and urgency as I feel different stages have different modes. Hurry up and wait, something I am very familiar with. The goal is always to move beyond concept models and create functional prototypes that can be tested, improved, and eventually produced into a solid MVP
